Who We Are

Starts With Soap is a 501(c)3 charitable organization that works to provide equitable education to our nation’s most underfunded schools by providing soap and other necessary materials.

From campaigns online to in person, we spread awareness about the lack of basic necessities in schools around the world.

Approach

Steps to Start with Soap.

        We recognize that change starts at the grassroots level, and we’ve organized our charity with this in mind.

        High schools around the nation have Starts With Soap chapters that partner with an underfunded school to act as a supplier for soap, as well as other unmet necessities.

        The activities of these high school and college chapters are coordinated and overseen by a team of regional executives and regional managers.

        The activities of regional executives and managers by our national leadership. Our national leadership team also manages fundraising, marketing, and outreach operations on a national level.

Mission

Ensure that all students get a shot at success by fostering equitable education in our nation’s most underfunded schools, providing soap, as well as other basic necessities. It all starts with soap.

  • Impetus

    Over 16,000 schools nationwide are on the lower end of the socioeconomic scale still suffer from the lack of basic public health resources such as soap and soap dispensers. This issue not only affects the quality of an educational institution but also the safety and healthiness of its members as they are unable to prevent sickness from sanitation related health hazards.

  • Purpose

    Our purpose is to eliminate threats to the healthiness of students due to underfunded facilities and lack of sanitation resources in educational institutions across the nation.

  • Goals

    Our goal is to supply local communities with the necessary resources and funds to ensure proper sanitation levels and ensure that every student has an equal shot at success, no matter where they go to school.

  • Execution

    We plan to align with the United Way organization to create a network of supporters and ambassadors throughout local communities and then distribute the collected funds to respective communities on a need-based system. The majority of our advertising is done through word-of-mouth, such as setting up initiatives on college campuses, and over social media, where we currently maintain a Facebook, Instagram and Twitter page.

  • Fundraising & Promotion

    Our funding comes from a combination of obtaining grants and fundraising privately. The majority of our advertising is done through word-of-mouth, such as setting up initiatives on college campuses, and over social media, where we currently maintain a Facebook, Instagram and Twitter page.

Corridor of Shame

Along the I-95 corridor in rural South Carolina, nearly 132,000 students attend schools that are underfunded to the point that their status and condition are illegal. This neglect has been contested and stalled in the South Carolina Supreme Court for nearly 20 years, and the delay in legal matters has been forcing an educational burden on the students who reside in the Corridor.

Currently, students are suffering every day and unable to reap the benefits of a strong education. Starts with Soap is currently working with a South Carolina-based NGO, Student Space, to reduce the plight by providing vital supplies to schools within the Corridor of Shame. Our goal is to target multiple schools in the area and assess the true needs of the schools by communicating with teachers and staff close to the heart of the issue. The project’s efforts are presently being led by Merrit of Student Space and Shrey, SWS Regional Executive.

For more information, email: sagarwal@startswithsoap.org

Detroit

SWS Regional Executive Rose is leading the institution of a new 90 minute reading program for Bennett Elementary in Detroit, MI, in order to provide students who come from low income backgrounds a stronger background in reading.

Through the project, SWS is developing an original reading curriculum in conjunction with Betsy A. VanDeusen, Ph.D Department Chairperson, Teacher Education and Professional Development at Central Michigan University. A team of SWS Executives has and will continue to develop a curriculum for all third grade students, including such components as vocabulary enrichment, passage comprehension, and more. The progress of the program will be monitored directly through SWS interviews within the school, student assessments, and periodic communication with the school. The program is set to begin at the school in Fall 2016 and will be monitored by the University of Michigan SWS Chapter.

For more information, email: rwong@startswithsoap.org

Atlanta

SWS is returning to the source of our inspiration, Parklane Elementary School, in Atlanta. All students attending the primary school participate in the Free and Reduced Lunch Program, indicating that each is of a low-income background and doesn’t often receive a number of opportunities to grow from. SWS Regional Executive Alex has been communicating with the school to help them install a STEM Education Learning Lab by Fall 2016. The station will be constructed using a Makey Makey compatible product, encouraging direct student engagement with technological and scientific concepts. We are currently seeking out a constant SWS Representative in the Atlanta area to monitor progress of the project.

For more information, email: azhang@startswithsoap.org

Indianapolis

Living in the age of advancement, we’re all quite familiar with the integration that technology has in our daily lives, especially in learning environments. Starts with Soap is addressing this concern head on with Computer Literacy for Indy, under the direction of SWS Regional Executive Vikas. The program is aimed towards advancing equitable computer literacy education among students of the Indianapolis Public School Districts via funding for the Edna Martin Christian Center. The Center currently serves 300 unduplicated students from these districts by providing tutoring services, after-school care, and supplementary educational resources. Our goal is to provide a set of computers to the Center, equipped with learning software that will teach both staff and students the workings of the technology. We are working alongside a nationally-recognized software company to arrange for the implementation of Computer Literacy for Indy. Follow along as we make a difference in the lives of countless students!

For more information, email: vmaturi@startswithsoap.org

West Virginia

SSWS is planning to help the victims of the recent West Virginia Floods under the lead of SWS Regional Executive Caroline. Our mission is to help rebuild schools through the re-implementation of basic supplies once the media has left and the real clean up begins. Caroline has been in contact with a West Virginia school in need of restock prior to the start of the school year, and SWS will follow up with a project with the intention of focusing on the school’s most demonstrated need. Our efforts will culminate through a three-pronged approach targeting fundraising for supplies while engendering awareness in our communities, both state- and nationwide!
